The mechanic

Good days build up.
Bad days cost you tomorrow.

Three ideas that make your daily number trustworthy. Fixed expenses are completely separate — what rolls forward is only what's genuinely yours to spend.

01 · Core mechanic

Daily
Rollover

Unspent money carries to tomorrow. Overspent money comes out of tomorrow. Both directions, automatically. Every day is connected — no fresh starts, no pretending.

02 · Clarity

Daily &
Fixed

Fixed holds rent, loans, subscriptions — set once, never touched by the rollover. Daily is what's genuinely left. When Spendly says $94, your rent is already covered.

03 · Flexibility

Weekend
Mode

Set a higher daily budget for weekends. If your Saturdays cost more than your Tuesdays, your budget should reflect that — not punish you for it.

How the rollover really works

The math is honest.
In both directions.

↑

You spend $50 on a $72 daily budget.

You saved $22. Tomorrow starts at $72 + $22 = $94. No ceremony, no transfers. It just happens automatically.

↓

You spend $95 on a $72 daily budget.

You overspent $23. Tomorrow starts at $72 − $23 = $49. The math is honest. No reset button. That's exactly the point.

This is different from every other budgeting app. Most apps let a bad week disappear on the 1st of the month. Spendly doesn't offer that escape — but every good day is genuinely rewarded, not quietly absorbed into a pool that resets anyway.
